Hello, World! — About Me

Hi, everyone. Let me introduce myself. I'm MochibagelBaby. I'm a self-taught developer and I love coding so much. I can code every day :D.

Though there is one thing I love more than coding! It is my most beloved boyfriend. He is my everything and my entire world. His love is the reason I smile every day, the genuine happiness I feel, the energy for me to get up every morning, and the inspiration for me to do great things and be the best version of myself.

Whoops, I digressed. I just want everyone to know how much I love him. Now, let's get back to the topic of coding. ;D

I'm a Full-stack Developer specializing in web technologies. Most of my work is to develop web applications for businesses. I mostly use HTML, CSS, JavaScript, EJS, Node.js, and MySQL on my daily development.

My experience in coding started back since 2015 when I was first introduced to the world of digital technology by a professional Swedish developer who were my boss and business partner at the time. I got recruited into the company with my design skills and the ability come up with creative ideas. I happened to be able to use Adobe Photoshop and Adobe XD pretty well through years of self-learning and I could make websites and applications look beautiful and intuitive to use. And that was what my boss always emphasized to me and our customers, especially when we were getting in business. Because no matter how great your programs could do things or how awesome the technology behind it is, user interface (UI) was normally what stirs up the buy-ins from department heads and gets the project started. The customers were normally intrigued when they saw a friendly and beautiful design of user interface, how seamless everything runs on the web/mobile applications, and intuitive workflow which also involves user experience design (UX). All in all, UX/UI Design captured attention, captivated the buy-ins from executives and attracted investments.

Most of the time we grabbed customers' attention by modern and attractive design and when they saw what they like they are willing to talk about the technology behind it and finally got down to signing a contract.

However, our challenges did not end there. We normally had to devote ourselves into the project in a very strict timeline and tried to solve all the problems that came up along the way, with most of them being the compatibility between the customers' enterprise software and other technologies that they want to integrate. We had set milestones for how long each functionality was gonna take to develop and when it was supposed to be finished so we could estimate a project budget, timeline, completion, and delivery date. After we were finished with development we would have a final meeting with customers to present what we had done and let tech lead from the customers' company go through each specification and check if we have completed everything previously agreed upon. After everything was approved, we would receive final project payment. Then, we celebrate and continue on with other projects. Sound like a lot, right? Working in a project is very challenging and fun. :D

That's all for today. Much love, everyone and bye bye. 🙋🏻‍♀️
